<p><p>The hand percussion market comprises various types, each offering unique sound characteristics. Finger cymbals are small metallic discs played by striking them together, creating bright, piercing tones. Tambourines consist of a frame and jingles, producing a rhythmic shaking sound, ideal for various musical genres. Hand bells are tuned instruments that ring with a clear tone when struck, often used in ensembles. Chimes, made of metal or wood, produce melodic sounds when struck. Other instruments in this category include shakers and claps, expanding the range of rhythmic expression.</p></p>
