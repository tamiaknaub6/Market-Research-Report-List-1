<p><p>The semiconductive ceramics market is categorized into two main types: two-component and multi-component systems. Two-component ceramics typically consist of a primary semiconductor material combined with a secondary phase to enhance specific properties, offering advantages like improved conductivity and temperature stability. In contrast, multi-component ceramics involve a combination of three or more materials, allowing for a broader range of electrical, thermal, and mechanical properties. These diverse formulations enable tailored applications in electronics, energy storage, and sensor technologies.</p></p>
