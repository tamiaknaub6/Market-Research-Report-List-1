<p><p>The edible hydrogenated oils market comprises two main types: partially hydrogenated oils and fully hydrogenated oils. Partially hydrogenated oils contain trans fats, created by adding hydrogen to liquid oils, which can enhance shelf life and stability but are linked to health risks. In contrast, fully hydrogenated oils undergo complete hydrogenation, resulting in a saturated fat product with no trans fats, offering a more stable and healthier alternative for cooking and food manufacturing. Both types serve distinct culinary and commercial purposes.</p></p>
