<p><p>The car steering shaft market includes three main types: Rack and Pinion, Worm Crank Pin, and Recirculating Ball. Rack and Pinion systems are compact, precise, and widely used in modern vehicles for direct steering response. Worm Crank Pin systems, common in older or heavy-duty vehicles, offer durability and mechanical advantage but are less precise. Recirculating Ball systems, found in trucks and SUVs, provide smooth operation and reduced friction, making them suitable for larger vehicles requiring robust steering mechanisms. Each type caters to specific vehicle needs and performance requirements.</p></p>
