<p><p>The single crystal diamond market is primarily divided into two types: HPHT (High Pressure, High Temperature) and CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition). HPHT involves mimicking the natural formation of diamonds under extreme pressure and temperature, producing high-quality gems for industrial and decorative use. In contrast, CVD utilizes carbon-rich gases to synthesize diamonds in a controlled environment, allowing for precise customization and scalability. Both methods serve diverse applications, including electronics, optics, and gemstones, driving market growth and innovation.</p></p>
